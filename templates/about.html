Accountability: About This Data Set

The dataset is originally generated and maintained by National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB), a highly-accountable institue. The dataset describes the incidents and accidents of Boeing 737 from 2002 to 2012. It consists of 242 rows and 23 columns(after cleaned). Some coloumns still contain incomplete data after cleaned. I remain them just for reference. And some incomplete columns from the original data are removed by me due to too much incompleteness or redundancy. The data is very accountable. As we mentioned in the home page, we can get some cool analysis from the complete part of this data set.

Where did this data come from?

I fetch the date from the Google Fusion Table. The data is sourced from National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB). NTSB is an independent U.S. government investigative agency responsible for civil transportation accident investigation. In this role, the NTSB investigates and reports on aviation accidents and incidents, certain types of highway crashes, ship and marine accidents, pipeline incidents, and railroad accidents. The original data from the google fusion table is here.

Who has access to it?

The NTSB's database is publicly accesible. We can access the database directly through the internet. And the data set I use in the byte is publicly accessible on google fusion table app.
